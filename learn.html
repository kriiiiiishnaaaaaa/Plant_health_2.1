📚 Plant Disease Library

Learn about common plant diseases, symptoms, and how to treat them.

🍂 Leaf Blight

Fungal

Brown, water-soaked lesions that spread rapidly across the leaf surface. Commonly affects rice, tomato, and wheat.

Symptoms

Yellowing edges, brown patches, wilting, premature leaf drop.

Treatment

  • • Apply copper-based fungicide
  • • Remove infected leaves
  • • Avoid overhead watering
⚠️ Moderate Risk

⬛ Powdery Mildew

Fungal

White powdery coating on leaf surfaces. Thrives in dry, warm conditions with high humidity.

Symptoms

White powder on leaves, curling, yellowing, stunted growth.

Treatment

  • • Spray baking soda + water mix
  • • Apply neem oil weekly
  • • Improve air circulation
⚠️ Mild Risk

🔴 Bacterial Spot

Bacterial

Small, water-soaked spots that turn dark brown or black. Common in tomatoes, peppers, and stone fruits.

Symptoms

Dark spots with yellow halos, greasy lesions, fruit blemishes.

Treatment

  • • Use copper bactericides
  • • Avoid wetting leaves while watering
  • • Remove and burn infected parts
🔴 High Risk

🟫 Early Blight

Fungal

Circular brown lesions with concentric rings. Major problem in tomatoes and potatoes.

Symptoms

Dark concentric rings on lower leaves, yellowing around lesions.

Treatment

  • • Apply chlorothalonil fungicide
  • • Mulch around plants
  • • Water at base, not foliage
⚠️ Moderate Risk

✅ Healthy Plant

No Disease

A healthy plant has vibrant green leaves, no spots, and firm structure.

Signs of Health

Deep green color, no yellowing, uniform texture, upright posture.

Maintenance Tips

  • • Water consistently (not over-water)
  • • Ensure adequate sunlight
  • • Use balanced fertilizer monthly
✅ No Risk

🟣 Leaf Rust

Fungal

Orange-brown pustules on the underside of leaves. Common in wheat, beans, and roses.

Symptoms

Rusty orange spore pustules, yellow spots on top surface, defoliation.

Treatment

  • • Apply propiconazole fungicide
  • • Destroy infected crop residue
  • • Use resistant varieties
🔴 High Risk
